# Can I Upgrade a Single-Hostname SSL Certificate to a Wildcard SSL Certificate?

No. It is not possible to upgrade a single-name SSL certificate to a [wildcard certificate](/articles/ssl-certificates-types/), even if they are issued by the same certificate authority. You must purchase a new wildcard certificate separately.

## Refund Policy {#refund}

If you decide a wildcard SSL certificate better fits your needs within 30 days of purchasing a single-name SSL certificate, [contact support](https://dnsimple.com/feedback) and we can refund your initial order so you are only charged for the wildcard.

## How to Order a Wildcard Certificate {#order}

- **Sectigo wildcard** - See [Ordering a Wildcard SSL Certificate](/articles/ordering-wildcard-certificate/). Covers all single-level subdomains plus the root domain. Costs $100/year.
- **Let's Encrypt wildcard** - See [Ordering a Let's Encrypt Certificate](/articles/ordering-lets-encrypt-certificate/). Covers all single-level subdomains (not the root domain). Free, but requires your domain to resolve with DNSimple.

For help choosing between single-name and wildcard coverage, see [Choosing the SSL Certificate Names](/articles/ssl-certificate-names/).
