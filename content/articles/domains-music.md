This article explains the requirements and special procedures for .MUSIC domain names.

## Registering a .MUSIC domain {#registering}

.MUSIC registrations are non-realtime. After registration, the registry sends a verification email to the registrant with instructions to complete identity and music industry validation at `id.music`.

The domain remains on `serverHold` until verification is completed.

## Registrant notice and acceptance {#notice}

The registry requires registrars and resellers to present an attestation notice to prospective registrants and retain proof of acceptance for up to 90 days.

CentralNic provides the `X-MUSIC-ACCEPT-TAC` extension (`0` or `1`) to record acceptance. The extension is currently available but not enforced.

## Reserved names {#reserved}

Some .MUSIC names are reserved. They can appear available in CentralNic, but registration requires an offline process and registry authorization.

## Transferring and changing registrant {#transfers}

Transfers use an auth code and require the domain to be unlocked.

When registrant details change, the new holder may also be required to complete the same identity and industry verification flow. If verification is not completed, the registry can suspend or revoke the domain.
